The story

Every subject asks you to read something, then write something. Between those two, everything scatters — PDFs in one place, notes in another, files with no system holding them together.

A literature essay, a research project, a history assignment, a week of revision — the path from a source to finished work is far more broken than it should be. We built Litranite to close that gap: one quiet place to read, mark, think and write. No accounts, no clutter, no noise.

Good to know

Questions, answered.

Is Litranite really free?+
Yes — the app you download today is completely free and open source, with no trial timer and no paywall. (Down the line we may add an optional, paid AI-powered version, but the core reading & note-taking app will always stay free.)
Where is my work stored?+
Entirely on your own device — no account, no cloud, no telemetry. Nothing leaves your computer. (Because it’s local, do keep your own backups; there’s a one-click export built in.)
What can I open and make?+
Drop in any PDF and it becomes a book on your shelf. Alongside it you get highlights in colour, a vocabulary bank, saved quotes with page numbers, free-form notes and folders, to-do lists, and mind-maps.
Which systems does it run on?+
macOS (Apple Silicon & Intel), Windows 10 & 11, and Linux — as an AppImage, .deb, or .rpm.
Why does my computer warn me on first open?+
Litranite is a free student project that isn’t code-signed yet, so macOS and Windows show a routine “unknown developer” notice. It’s not a virus — the step-by-step Mac and Windows guides show exactly what to click.
Who made it?+
Two students — Ayanaaya Waghmare and Tanissha Gunjal — who were tired of juggling PDFs, notes and vocab in a dozen places. Read the story →
